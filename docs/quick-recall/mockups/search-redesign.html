Quick Recall / search redesign

Research-backed: List + Detail Pane

The evidence-driven synthesis, deliberately simple. Microsoft Recall's shipped answer to mixed-media search is exactly two modality headers and a single per-app filter — mirrored here as SCREEN / AUDIO sections plus one "All apps" dropdown, with Recall's thin scrubbable timeline (hover a dot for a thumbnail preview) replacing all date grouping and facet chips. Rows follow Raycast's anatomy — thumb left, app strong + window title muted, one snippet line, accessories right — backed by a Raycast-style detail pane whose duplicated accessories are stripped from the selected row. Recall's postage-stamp thumbnails were its documented scanning failure, so thumbnails are 150px and legible; audio follows Screenpipe/Granola as attributed transcript text with a waveform glyph, never a fake visual. Two headers and one-line rows are the NN/g layer-cake scanning pattern: eyes sweep headings first, then rows. Note: selection = preview-in-detail-pane is a DELIBERATE semantic change from today's window, where selecting a result opens it in the main window and closes Quick Recall.
