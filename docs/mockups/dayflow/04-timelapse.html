◆ Annotations · 04 Timelapse Receipt

What it does

Dayflow stitches each card's screenshots into a timelapse video. Mnema already stores the actual frames — so the receipt is a scrubable frame strip over the card's time range: play at 8×, drag the scrubber, or click a filmstrip thumb. Green ticks above the spine mark the frames the engine cited as evidence (the bright tick is the headline frame, which doubles as the poster).

But an Activity's evidence is frames and spoken audio — the engine cites both. So the track carries a second channel: lavender ticks below the spine mark the cited Audio Transcription Spans. Click one and the single playhead plays that segment's real audio at 1× while the frame viewer runs the same window, clocked by the audio — a bounded "relive this moment" clip. Hovering a tick shows the cited transcript (the clip's label) so you know what you'll hear. An Activity grounded only in audio (a call with the screen off) plays as a bounded audio player instead of lying "footage expired".

Each cited moment also shows who spoke — you (microphone) vs the other side (system audio), plus a recognized name when known — read-only, resolved live by id so a voice you name later shows its real name (never a frozen "Speaker 2"). This is where you catch the engine's speaker-blind misattributions ("someone spoke to me, the card says I said it"); fixing them hands off — naming a voice stays Timeline, a wrong card routes to the Activity's own Dismiss. The blindness itself gets its root fix in source-aware derivation (ADR 0050 — a separate follow-up).

This is the proof-and-bounded-inspection layer for the whole Journal: every AI-written sentence is one interaction from its evidence — from "see the cited pixel" to "hear the cited moment", all scoped to the one Activity's span. "Open in Timeline →" hands off anything wider: the raw frame, or (for audio) the full transcript, speaker turns, and unbounded scrub in Timeline's audio drawer. The playhead pill reads wall-clock time ("10:04 AM"), not elapsed. The line the receipt holds is bounded, not silent (ADR 0049).

Design provenance: Dayflow's review scrubber (wall-clock playhead pill; its poster is the middle frame — ours does better with the engine's cited headline frame), Loom (chapter ticks on the track + denser thumbnail sampling near markers), QuickTime (hold a control for frame-by-frame precision).

How it works

Open from any activity card. The modal:

  • fetches frame ids with the existing list_frames over [startedAtMs, endedAtMs]
  • renders frames lazily via the existing get_frame_preview — playback = swapping previews on requestAnimationFrame, with lookahead prefetch into a small LRU (~40 frames) and decodes outside the window cancelled on scrub; the playhead moves via transform with no transitions (that combination is what makes raw frames feel like video)
  • overlays frame ticks (above) and audio ticks (below) from user_context_activity_evidence — subject_type splits them; is_headline is the poster frame / lead spoken moment
  • a cited audio tick plays the bounded clip via the existing get_audio_segment_media at 1×, driving the frame index off started_at + audio.currentTime — same frame-swap trick, now clocked by an <audio> element instead of the rAF timer; stops at the segment end
  • "Open in Timeline" carries a frame id or an audio segment id — the frontend handoff slot (pending-focus) opens Timeline's audio drawer on the segment when there's no frame

No video is ever encoded — scrubbing the frame strip gives the timelapse feel with zero media pipeline. The viewer shows a frame-shaped skeleton while previews load; evidence ticks explain themselves via hover tooltip (shown) instead of a permanent caption. Keyboard: ←/→ steps frames, space plays, esc closes.

Net-new code

  • One frontend modal, e.g. apps/desktop/src/lib/insights/ActivityReceipt.svelte, opened from the Journal card's "receipt" affordance
  • Reuses the frame-preview plumbing in apps/desktop/src/lib/frame-preview.ts and the commands already registered in apps/desktop/src-tauri/src/lib.rs (app_infra::list_frames, app_infra::frame_preview::get_frame_preview, app_infra::get_timeline_window_around_frame)

Not built: no new tables, no new Tauri commands, no new worker, and deliberately no mp4 stitching/export (Dayflow's approach) — encoding adds an ffmpeg-shaped dependency for something the frame strip already delivers. The audio clip reuses get_audio_segment_media (already Timeline's) — still no new backend. Export can come later if anyone asks. Evidence refs (frame and audio_segment, incl. is_headline) arrive pre-hydrated on the Journal's range read — the receipt adds no data plumbing of its own.

Data it uses

  • Raw frames per capture segment (app, window title, URL, OCR metadata ride along — shown in the frame-meta chips, same data the Timeline overlay shows today)
  • user_context_activity_evidence — cited frame and audio_segment refs + the is_headline flag
  • audio segment media + its redacted transcript via get_audio_segment_media / get_audio_segment (the caption serves the same redacted text Timeline shows; playback carries the same posture as Timeline already does for that segment)

Privacy: excluded apps never produced frames, so they simply don't appear; OCR redaction applied at capture is what the preview serves. The asymmetry from ADR 0029 cuts the other way here — frames are subject to Retention Cleanup while the card is not — so the expired state above is guaranteed, not an edge case. Delete Recent Capture removes whole overlapping segments, which can blank part of a receipt: missing segments render as dead zones on the scrubber track, with the surviving frames still scrubable.

Grill outcomes (Jul 5, docs/user-context/CONTEXT.md + ADR 0049): the receipt is bounded evidence playback and inspection of one Activity — it now plays cited audio as bounded 1× synced clips, reversing the Jul 3 "never grows audio playback" rule. The line moved from silent → audible to bounded → unbounded: OCR copy/download, frame export, cross-Activity navigation, and audio scrubbing past this Activity's cited moments still belong to Timeline, and "Open in Timeline" is the answer for those. Speakers are shown but not managed here (surface-and-handoff): who-spoke is read-only and late-bound by id, naming voices stays Timeline, and the engine's own speaker-blindness gets its root fix in source-aware derivation (ADR 0050, separate follow-up). Known accepted slop: get_frame_preview seeks by video_offset_ms, so pre-fix sidecars (captured before Jul 3 2026) show pixels ~16s off their tick — same fidelity as the Timeline, no receipt-specific handling.
