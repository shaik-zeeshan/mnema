# Ask AI threads complete in the background and resurrect from their transcript

> **Superseded in part by [ADR 0031](0031-quick-recall-and-chat-share-one-persistent-conversation-store.md):** this ADR's **disk-ephemerality** ("persists nothing to disk") no longer holds — **Quick Recall** conversations now persist to the shared conversation store so a thread can be opened/continued in the Insights **Chat** workspace. The in-memory background-completion / resurrect lifetime described below is unchanged and still applies.

**Quick Recall**'s **Ask AI Thread** no longer dies the moment the panel is dismissed. An **unseen** conversation — one still streaming, or finished but never looked at while the panel was focused — now **survives dismiss/blur** so a re-summon lands back on it, completes in the background while the webview is hidden, and stays readable. A finished-but-unseen thread keeps its resident PI helper for a bounded **30-minute unseen window**; past that the helper is released (the idle Node/PI process is reclaimed) but the rendered transcript stays intact and the thread is marked **expired**. A follow-up question on a **dead** thread (expired, or whose last turn errored — an error already kills the helper Rust-side) **resurrects** it: a fresh PI session is started and re-fed the prior question/answer turns as `priorTranscript`, continuing into the same visible transcript.

This **amends** [ADR 0026](0026-ask-ai-multi-turn-reuses-live-pi-session.md) in two ways. First, it extends the thread lifetime: ADR 0026 bounded a thread's PI session to the `IDLE_CLEAR_MS` (5s) hide/re-summon idle window and tore it down on any dismiss; an unseen outcome now persists through dismiss for up to 30 minutes of resident helper and indefinitely as a readable transcript while Quick Recall stays open. Second, it narrowly **reverses ADR 0026's rejection of transcript replay** — but only as a recovery path. ADR 0026 rejected "re-seed each turn into a fresh session, replaying the transcript" because it would re-send all prior turns *on every follow-up while a live session exists*. Resurrection replays the transcript **once, only when the live session is already gone**, where the alternative is losing the conversation entirely rather than paying redundant cost against a session that could have answered for free. Normal follow-ups against a live session still ride the resident session per ADR 0026 and send no transcript.

The disk-ephemerality posture of ADR 0026 is unchanged: the transcript lives only in the panel's in-memory frontend state, persists nothing to disk, and is torn down unconditionally on a full **Quick Recall** close (`onDestroy`) and at app exit. What changed is the in-memory lifetime, not disk persistence.

## "Seen" is the gate, not idle time

A conversation is **seen** once its last turn reached a terminal phase (`done` or `error`) **and** that terminal turn was rendered while the window was focused — even momentarily; glancing at a finished answer on re-summon counts. A still-streaming turn is never seen. Until seen, the conversation is **pending** and is exempt from both teardown paths: the 5s blur idle-clear (ADR 0026) and the dismiss-time cancel. Once seen, the conversation returns to ADR 0026's ordinary ephemeral rules (cleared on dismiss, 5s idle-clear on blur). The dismiss chokepoint in `windows.rs` (`dismiss_quick_recall_window`) therefore stops cancelling the resident PI session unconditionally and instead emits the dismiss event for the panel to decide; the panel keeps a pending conversation alive and only tears down a seen one or none.

## Considered Options

- **Background completion + transcript resurrection (chosen).** Keep an unseen conversation alive across dismiss so it finishes in the background and a re-summon lands back on it; bound a finished-but-unseen helper to 30 minutes, then release the process but keep the transcript; resurrect a dead thread on follow-up by re-feeding its Q/A transcript into a fresh session. This makes "ask, dismiss, come back" coherent without persisting anything to disk, and keeps the live-session reuse of ADR 0026 as the common path — replay is the exception, used only when there is no session to reuse.
- **Strict ADR 0026 lifetime (rejected).** Tear the thread down on every dismiss and at the 5s idle window. Rejected: dismissing the panel while an answer is still streaming (or before reading it) silently throws away the in-flight cloud work and the answer, which reads as a bug for a launcher the user flicks in and out of.
- **Persist threads to disk and offer a recent-threads list (rejected/deferred).** A durable conversation store would survive a full Quick Recall close. Rejected here: it reintroduces exactly the persisted-history privacy surface ADR 0026 and [ADR 0025](0025-quick-recall-search-syntax-uses-launcher-native-autocomplete.md) deliberately kept out of Quick Recall. Background completion gets the practical "come back to my answer" benefit without a store.
- **Resurrect by reconnecting to the old PI child (rejected).** Once the 30-minute window releases the helper, the Node/PI process is gone; there is nothing to reconnect to. Replaying the transcript into a fresh session is the only way to continue without holding the process open indefinitely.

## Consequences

- **A resident PI helper after dismiss is expected, not a leak.** An unseen conversation's Node child stays alive while the panel is hidden so the stream keeps applying deltas in the background. It is bounded two ways: the 30-minute unseen cap (frontend-owned `ASK_UNSEEN_EXPIRY_MS`, fixed, no setting), and app exit / `onDestroy`, which still tear it down unconditionally. The 30-minute timer is armed only for a `done`-while-unseen turn (an `error` already released the helper Rust-side) and disarms the moment the conversation is seen, is replaced by a fresh ask, or is reset.
- **Expiry releases the process but not the transcript.** When the unseen window fires, the panel cancels the resident helper through the ordinary `ask_ai_cancel` path **without** nulling the thread id or clearing the turns, so the answer stays readable and the thread is marked `expired`. This is distinct from the Escape/cancel teardown, which discards the thread.
- **Resurrection is transparent and one-way into the same transcript.** A follow-up on a dead thread starts a fresh `ask_ai_start` (new `conversationId`) carrying `priorTranscript`, swaps the active id, and appends the new turn beneath the preserved prior turns. The stale-thread guard (the `conversationId` check in the stream listeners) keeps stragglers from the old session off the new turn. The only user-facing cue is a subtle muted hint above the composer ("Session paused/ended — your next question resumes from this transcript"); there is no confirmation dialog.
- **Only answered Q/A is replayed — never tool logs or Answer Sources.** `priorTranscript` carries the question and answer text of completed (`done`, non-empty-answer) turns only. Errored or never-completed turns are dropped. **Answer Sources**, per-turn tool activity, and seeded broker context are not re-sent, keeping the replayed payload to established conversation content.
- **The replayed context block is size-capped Rust-side.** `ask_ai.rs` formats `priorTranscript` into a clearly-delimited "prior conversation from an earlier session" block prepended to the first prompt, bounded by `ASK_AI_PRIOR_TRANSCRIPT_CHAR_CAP` (12k chars). Trimming drops the **oldest** whole turns first; if even the single most recent turn exceeds the cap, that turn's text is truncated with a marker so the block always fits. This keeps an old, long conversation from blowing up a resurrected first turn's token budget. The frontend sends the full list and lets the host trim.
- **Seeding is never polluted by the transcript.** Resurrection sends the bare new question as `seedQuery`; the transcript rides only the first prompt, never the broker seed search. A normal start with no `priorTranscript` (absent or empty) behaves exactly as before — fully backward-compatible.
- **More captured-derived content leaves per resurrection than per live follow-up.** Replaying prior answers re-sends earlier turn text to the cloud, which ADR 0022's minimize-what-leaves principle argues against. This is accepted as bounded (≤12k chars, answered-Q/A only, once per resurrection rather than per turn) and as the cost of continuing a conversation whose session is gone; the redaction/retention boundary of [ADR 0022](0022-ask-ai-sends-redacted-capture-context-to-cloud-agent.md) and [ADR 0024](0024-ask-ai-uses-pi-tool-shim-over-installed-runtime.md) is otherwise unchanged.
- **Every teardown still routes through cancel.** Dismiss-of-a-seen-thread, expiry, Escape, and app exit all release the helper through `ask_ai_cancel`/`cancelActiveAsk` so no orphaned Node child outlives the panel. The dismiss handler in `windows.rs` now defers the decision to the panel rather than cancelling unconditionally.

## Out of scope

- Persisted chat history, a thread/recent-threads list, or resuming a thread across a full **Quick Recall** close (`onDestroy`) or app restart. Resurrection works only within a still-open panel's in-memory transcript.
- A user-configurable unseen-window duration; the 30-minute cap is a fixed constant.
- Replaying tool activity, **Answer Sources**, or seeded broker context into a resurrected session.
- A per-turn "stop generating" that keeps the thread alive — still deferred per [ADR 0026](0026-ask-ai-multi-turn-reuses-live-pi-session.md).
- Any change to redaction, retention, broker scope, or the `reference_captures` presentation contract.
